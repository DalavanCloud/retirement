How do we generate your estimates?

We base your estimates on current formulas from the Social Security Administration. We use the Social Security Administration’s Quick Calculator to produce a rough estimate of your retirement benefit amount at what’s known as your “full retirement age.” The “full retirement age” is the age at which Social Security will pay 100 percent of your scheduled benefit under current law, and it is based on the year you were born. We use this rough estimate to produce the estimates for all other ages by applying the appropriate monthly early claiming reductions or delayed claiming credits.

These are rough estimates

We do not access your actual Social Security earnings record, which is based on your highest 35 years of earnings. Instead, we use your self-reported highest year of earnings to recreate your entire Social Security earnings record using the default assumptions about wage growth as the Social Security Administration’s Quick Calculator.

Your estimates may be different from those produced by other tools from the Social Security Administration

The estimates that you see on our tool may be different from the estimates shown in the Social Security Administration’s my Social Security accounts, Retirement Estimator, or the paper statement that you may receive from Social Security because these tools use your actual earnings record to generate an estimate. The estimate shown in this tool may also be different from the Social Security Administration’s Quick Calculator for early and later claiming ages because our estimates only show the effects of claiming at different ages with no additional adjustments such as cost of living adjustments or the extra effects on the benefits for working after age 62.

The tool may not be able to provide you an estimate in some situations

The tool does not provide estimates of retirement benefits for consumers under the age of 22 or over 70. Additionally, the tool cannot provide an estimate if the amount entered is too low to generate estimates.

As you approach retirement, contact the Social Security Administration for an estimate based on your actual earning history

Your estimate will be more accurate if it is based on your own actual earnings record, especially if you have had fluctuations in your income over your career. To get an estimate based on your earnings record, you may want to open a my Social Security account or visit the Social Security Administration’s Retirement Estimator.
